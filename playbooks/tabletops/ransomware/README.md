# Ransomware Tabletop Kit (Cities/Counties)

A facilitator-ready tabletop exercise kit for running a 60–90 minute discussion on ransomware impact, containment, communications, restoration decisions, public-service disruption, and leadership pressure.

## Purpose
Help public-sector leadership, IT, security, communications, and business owners rehearse the decisions that matter when ransomware or destructive encryption affects critical services, shared systems, or public-facing operations.

## Best for
- city/county IT leadership
- security leads
- server, endpoint, identity, or backup owners
- executive sponsors
- communications / PIO
- finance, legal, records, and operations stakeholders where available

## What’s included
- `facilitator-guide.md` — how to run the exercise
- `participant-brief.md` — scenario brief and objectives
- `injects.md` — timed injects and decision points
- `evaluator-worksheet.md` — observer/evaluator notes sheet
- `hotwash-template.md` — after-action capture template

## Recommended use
1. Read the facilitator guide.
2. Tailor the scenario to your real environment and critical services.
3. Invite leadership + IT + comms + critical business owners.
4. Run the scenario in 60–90 minutes using the rolling timestamps in `injects.md` to keep pacing consistent for the facilitator.
5. Convert findings into updates for backup validation, containment authority, restoration sequencing, communications, cyber insurance notice, reporting/notification verification, and third-party/vendor coordination.

## Good-enough outcome
At the end of the exercise, the team should be able to answer:
- Who leads containment, restoration, and executive escalation?
- What systems or services get restored first?
- What backup assumptions are actually validated?
- What can be said internally or publicly, and by whom?
- What decisions require legal, insurance, law-enforcement, reporting/notification, or outside-partner involvement?

## Optional adversary flavor
This kit can lightly reuse the same fictional adversary framing used in the KEV tabletop:
- **Spectral Raccoon / APT-1337**

Use it sparingly to create realism around uncertain attribution, exfiltration rumors, and communications pressure — not to distract from the ransomware decision-making core of the exercise.
